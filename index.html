About Me

I’m an MSBS student at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, an EMT, and an aspiring MD/PhD candidate. I graduated from NYU Tandon in 2025, majoring in Biomolecular Engineering with a minor in Mathematics. At NYU Engineering, and later NYU Langone, I generated embedded-sensing synthetic skin and studied DYRK1A–APP interactions in Down syndrome mice. Afterwards, I interned at Columbia Engineering, studying geothermal heat reactors and passive tracking solar. Later at Pfizer Vaccine R&D, I developed CNN protein mutagenesis systems, viral sequence databasing, and experimental remote automation. Now, as a graduate student in the Castellano Lab, I investigate ApoE3/4 plasma proteomics in aging and design machine-learning morphology pipelines.
